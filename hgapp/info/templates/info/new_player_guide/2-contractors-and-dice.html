The Starlight Motel struck me as the kinda place bad things happened. Not even that gorgeous twilight sky--no sun or moon in sight, casting everything above and below in shades of violet-- not even it could turn this parking-lot motel into a place that felt safe. 

I climbed the steps to the second floor and knocked on the door of room 231. No answer. The windows were black.

“You’re looking for Bradley.” The voice came from behind: a pale teen wearing a pleather duster and watches on both wrists.

“Are you here on Contract?”

“You could say that.” 

“Then we are working together. I am Five.” 

“You look a little older than that.” 

“That is my name. I am seventeen years old. I think.”

“Uh huh. Look, kid, I don’t really need a sidekick.”

Five stared at me. “You must be new. We will need all the help we can get.”

“You’ve done this before?” 

Five nodded.

“So you have. . . powers.”

Another nod. 

“Okay. I’ll work with you. But you better not get in my way.”

“Same to you.”

Contractors without a single Victory are called Newbies.
Novice Contractors (1-9 Victories) can participate in Contracts with Newbies and other Novices.
Seasoned Contractors (10+ Victories) can only play with other Seasoned and Veteran (25+ Victories) Contractors.
Only Veteran Contractors can participate in solo Contracts.

Then came the click-clacking of cowboy boots traveling up the stairwell. The man attached had one arm, a revolver on each hip, and a mustache that would put Yosemite Sam to shame. His sheriff’s star winked at me. 

“Great. A cop,” I said to myself. 

Five nodded. “Bo.” 

The man approached, face cracking into a smile. “Five! Here to pick up a new timepiece?” 

“No. We are on a Contract.”

“We’ve gotta work on your sense of humor, Five.” He turned to me and held out his hand. “Looks like this is the team. The name’s Bo Perkins. Pleased to meet you.” 

“Fuck off, pig.”

Bo’s smile disappeared. “I don’t like that attitude. Not one bit.” 

Five stiffened. “She is new.”

“Ah. Well, little miss sunshine, as long as we’re bein’ candid with each other, I don’t think I like you either. You look like a punk, and I’m sure I could find enough dirt to put you away for a long time. But unless you signed a very different contract than I did, I think we ought to put aside our differences and work together.”

The six Attributes represent your intrinsic strengths and weaknesses.
Attributes are rated 1-5. A rating of 2 is the human average. A rating of 5 is world-class.
Your Attribute values determine many of your other stats, such as movement speed and mental fortitude.
Abilities represent your learned skills and knowledge.
Even a single rank in an Ability indicates some training or experience in that field. 
Abilities are only used in skill checks.
